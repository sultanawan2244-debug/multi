What Is an Image Histogram?

An image histogram is a graphical representation of the tonal distribution in a digital image. It plots the number of pixels for each tonal value, from 0 (black) on the left to 255 (white) on the right. By showing how pixels are distributed across these levels, histograms reveal crucial information about exposure, contrast, and color balance. Our free online tool generates separate histograms for the Red, Green, and Blue channels plus a luminance histogram, giving you comprehensive insight into your image's color characteristics.

Why Use Our Histogram Tool?

Photographers use histograms to evaluate exposure: a histogram pushed to the left indicates underexposure (too dark), while one pushed right indicates overexposure (too bright). A well-exposed image typically has a histogram spread across the full range. Designers use histograms to assess color balance: if one channel is significantly different from others, the image may have a color cast. Our tool makes this analysis instant and visual, helping you make better decisions about exposure correction, contrast adjustment, and color grading.
